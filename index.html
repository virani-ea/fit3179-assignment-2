Mapping Healthcare Access in Victoria

Are hospitals located where people live? How do socioeconomic factors influence healthcare equity?

1. Population Density and Hospital Locations

It is evident that the majority of hospitals are positioned in or near high-density regions, supporting health service accessibility in populous urban zones. However, the map also reveals large, low-density areas with few or no hospitals, raising questions about healthcare access for rural communities. These spatial insights prompt further investigation into service availability and equity outside major cities.

2. How does Victoria compare to other states?

This chart compares Australian states and territories by the number of public hospitals per 100,000 residents—a basic indicator of potential healthcare access.

While densely populated regions like NSW and Victoria have the highest absolute numbers of hospitals, smaller states (such as Tasmania and South Australia) have more hospitals per capita, improving local access.

ACT stands out for its uniquely low ratio, largely due to its city-state structure. This perspective highlights regional inequalities in infrastructure, which can have implications for healthcare delivery and resilience.
